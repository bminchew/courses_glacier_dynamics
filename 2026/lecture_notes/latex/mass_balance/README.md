# Glacier Mass Balance Processes

Mass balance is the fundamental quantity linking climate to ice dynamics. This lecture covers how mass is added to and removed from glaciers, how snow transforms into ice, and how the balance between accumulation and ablation determines glacier geometry and flow. The balance velocity concept connects climate forcing directly to the velocity field, and discrepancies between observed and balance velocities diagnose whether glaciers are gaining or losing mass.
