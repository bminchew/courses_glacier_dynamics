# Ice Dynamics I: Strain Rates, Simple Shear, and Ice Sheet Profiles

This lecture bridges from stress analysis and mass balance to the first complete dynamics prediction: ice sheet surface profiles. It introduces the strain-rate tensor, states Glen's flow law, solves the parallel-slab problem to obtain the fundamental velocity profile, and then applies the shallow-ice approximation to derive steady-state ice sheet profiles (Vialov) from first principles. The perfectly plastic limit and comparison with observed profiles round out the lecture.

## Outline

1. Motivation and review (connecting to previous lectures)
2. Strain rates and the velocity gradient tensor
   - Velocity gradient, strain-rate tensor, spin tensor
   - Physical interpretation, incompressibility, effective strain rate
3. Glen's flow law (stated, not derived)
4. Simple shear: the parallel slab
   - Stress distribution from a free-body diagram
   - Velocity profile derivation
   - Depth-averaged velocity and flux
5. The ice sheet as a viscous gravity current
   - Shallow-ice approximation (SIA)
   - Coupling to the continuity equation
   - The Vialov profile and insensitivity to parameters
6. The perfectly plastic limit
7. Comparison with observations (interior vs. margins)
8. Summary
